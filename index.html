History of Seattle

Seattle was founded in 1851. It was named after a local Native American tribe chieftain. In 1889, a large amount of the city was burned down. However, against all odds, no lives were lost. The next day, the businesses were back up and running in tents. The burnt down city did have one advantage though. It prevented a problem with sewage flowing back up at high tide because they built on top of the ruins. In 1896, the Klondike Gold Rush started in Alaska. However, in order to get to Alaska, many people went through Seattle. By selling goods needed for gold mining, Seattle grew a lot.

Travel Info

You can stay in hotels. Most chains can be found there.
The budget depends on the person. According to ChampionTraveler, the average person spends $217-$419 dollars per day. However, they estimate a budget traveler can spend $112-$225 a day. That same site says flights are cheapest in September and October.
That is not surprising because if you go in those months or November, do not expect to see any blue sky. If you want blue sky, come during the summer when it is bright, sunny, and 70 degrees fahrenheit. The rest of the year is characterized by gray clouds and a near constant light drizzle. In the winter, the weather tends to be about 20-30 degrees fahrenheit. It snows only once or twice a year. However, if you are there during a snow storm, beware. Everything could shut down. The city has no idea how to deal with snow and barely any snow can grind the city to a halt. Just ask people who lived there last year during the so called "snowmaggedon". A couple inches of snow a couple times in Februrary caused there to be no school for an entire month. The governer even declared a state of emergency. Besides the city's incompetence, I would say the weather is much better in the winter but most people might disagree. Keep in mind however, A/C isn't nearly as important because most of the year it is cold. This causes summer, despite being not nearly as hot as other places, in certain buildings to be an inescapable nightmare, even to someone from Arizona, though this source might have adapted to not living in AZ for a couple of years.
